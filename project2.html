Manual Tote Cleaner

Overview

This manually operated mechanism inverts totes to a precise angle and height so that sanitation staff can pressure‑wash the interiors without heavy lifting. It offers a streamlined alternative to lifting and ensures the tote’s rim remains off the floor, improving hygiene while avoiding the complexity and cost of full automation.

Role

As an engineering intern, I led the concept development in SolidWorks, from the structural framework to the lifting mechanism, while coordinating with senior engineers and suppliers. My responsibilities included sizing the drive motor through torque calculations, incorporating counterweights to balance loads, accommodating multiple tote dimensions, and selecting corrosion‑resistant bearings for wash‑down environments. Throughout the project I worked with vendors to ensure manufacturability and cost effectiveness.

Tools Used

SolidWorks with weldment and sheet‑metal features for design; analytical calculations for mass properties and torque; and mechanical design techniques such as counterbalance weight systems and wash‑down compliant bearings.

Outcome / Impact

This design illustrates how thoughtful mechanical engineering can yield a robust, lower‑cost alternative to automation. It enhances worker safety by eliminating heavy lifting and improves sanitation, while maintaining cost efficiency and demonstrating the importance of exploring alternative mechanisms and design ideas.
