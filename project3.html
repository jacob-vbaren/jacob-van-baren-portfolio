Controlled Hopper

Overview

The controlled hopper is engineered to meter product flow through an adjustable opening using a rack‑and‑pinion actuated slide gate. Designed for freezing environments, the hopper’s geometry and surface finish prevent material from sticking even at sub‑zero temperatures, ensuring reliable dispensing.

Role

As an engineering intern, I led the mechanical design while working alongside seasoned engineers and production specialists. I performed the 3D modelling, dimensioning and tolerance stack‑ups, sourced the rack, pinion, gears and handle through McMaster‑Carr, and integrated them into a compact, robust assembly. I also verified the mechanism’s geometry through calculations and hands‑on testing with sample product.

Tools Used

SolidWorks for 3D modelling and kinematic analysis; component selection and specification; rack‑and‑pinion mechanism design; and bench testing of assembled components.

Outcome / Impact

The hopper’s slide gate offers multiple set points to precisely control product discharge. Testing in operational conditions validated that the chute angle and gate travel allow consistent flow while a positive lock on the handle prevents accidental movement. This design demonstrates my ability to integrate off‑the‑shelf components into a purpose‑built mechanism that meets stringent processing requirements.
